<p><p>The sustainable succinic acid market can be classified into two main types: Mannheimia and others. Mannheimia refers to the production of succinic acid using microorganisms, particularly Mannheimia succiniciproducens. This method involves the fermentation of renewable feedstocks, such as sugars or lignocellulosic biomass, to produce succinic acid. On the other hand, the "others" category entails alternative production methods, including chemical synthesis, electrochemical process, or thermochemical conversion. Both approaches aim to create succinic acid in an environmentally friendly and economically viable manner.</p></p>
