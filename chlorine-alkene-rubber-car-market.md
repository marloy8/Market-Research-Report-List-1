<p><p>Chlorine Alkene Rubber (CAR) is a type of synthetic rubber that is derived from the polymerization of a mixture of chlorine and ethylene. The rising demand for high-performance rubber materials, particularly in the automotive industry, is driving the market growth. CAR is widely used in the manufacturing of automotive components such as hoses, gaskets, seals, and belts due to its superior properties and durability. Additionally, the growing construction industry, especially in emerging economies, is further fueling the demand for CAR, as it is extensively used in roofing and waterproofing applications.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing demand for specialty rubber products in sectors such as electronics and healthcare is also contributing to the market growth.
