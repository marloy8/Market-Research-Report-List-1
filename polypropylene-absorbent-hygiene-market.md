<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The market for polypropylene absorbent hygiene products can be categorized into four types: spunbonded fabric, staples fabric, melt blown fabric, and composite fabric. Spunbonded fabric refers to a nonwoven fabric made by spinning and bonding polypropylene fibers together. Staples fabric involves the use of individual staple fibers that are laid down in a random arrangement and then bonded together. Melt blown fabric is created by extruding molten polypropylene to form fine fibers that are then cooled and bonded. Composite fabric combines different types of fabrics to enhance absorbency and overall performance.</p></p>
