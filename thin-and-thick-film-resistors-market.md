<p><p>Thin film resistors are resistors made by depositing a thin layer of resistive material on a ceramic or glass substrate. The resistive material used is typically a metal alloy such as nickel-chromium. These resistors are known for their precision and stability, making them suitable for applications that require high accuracy and low noise.</p><p>On the other hand, thick film resistors are resistors made by screen-printing a resistive paste on a substrate and then firing it to form a thick, stable film. The resistive paste used contains particles of conductive material such as ruthenium oxide or bismuth ruthenate. Thick film resistors are cost-effective and offer high power handling capabilities.</p><p>The market analysis of the thin and thick film resistors market suggests a positive outlook.
