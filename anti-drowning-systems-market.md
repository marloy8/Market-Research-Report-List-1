<p><p>Anti-drowning systems can be categorized into two main types: wearable and mounted systems. Wearable systems are specifically designed to be worn by individuals, such as life jackets or inflatable armbands. These devices provide buoyancy and keep the wearer afloat in water, reducing the risk of drowning. On the other hand, mounted systems are installed in pools, beaches, or other water bodies. These systems use various technologies like sensors, cameras, or alarms to monitor water conditions and detect potential drowning incidents, alerting nearby lifeguards or authorities for immediate rescues. Both types aim to enhance water safety and prevent drowning incidents.</p></p>
