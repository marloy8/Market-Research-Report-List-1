<p><p>An oil-immersed potential transformer is a type of instrument transformer that is used to measure the voltage in high voltage power transmission systems. It is immersed in oil for insulation purposes and to cool down the transformer. The primary winding of the transformer is connected in parallel with the circuit being measured, while the secondary winding is connected to measuring or protection devices.</p><p>The global oil-immersed potential transformer market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The market is driven by the increasing demand for electricity, especially in developing countries, and the growing focus on renewable energy sources. The need for efficient power transmission and distribution systems is also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the aging power infrastructure in many countries is creating a demand for the replacement of old potential transformers, driving the market growth. Moreover, the increasing investments in smart grids and digital substations are expected to fuel the demand for oil-immersed potential transformers.</p><p>However, the market is also facing challenges such as the increasing adoption of gas-insulated potential transformers, which offer better performance and reliability.
