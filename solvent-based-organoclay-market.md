<p><p>Solvent-based organoclay is a type of organoclay that is dispersed in solvent-based systems. It is available in three different market types: meal, powder, and superfine powder. Meal refers to larger particles with higher viscosity, suitable for applications such as adhesives and sealants. Powder has smaller particle size, offering improved rheological properties for uses in coatings and composites. Superfine powder has the smallest particle size, providing enhanced dispersibility, thixotropic behavior, and sag resistance, commonly utilized in printing inks, greases, and drilling fluids. These different types cater to specific industry requirements.</p></p>
