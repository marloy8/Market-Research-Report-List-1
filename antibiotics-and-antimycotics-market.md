<p><p>Antibiotics and antimycotics are two types of medications used to treat bacterial and fungal infections respectively. </p><p>Antibiotics are drugs used to kill or inhibit the growth of bacteria, hence eliminating the infection. They are widely used in the treatment of various bacterial infections such as pneumonia, urinary tract infections, and skin infections. On the other hand, antimycotics or antifungals are medications used to treat fungal infections, including yeast infections, athlete's foot, and ringworm. They work by targeting the fungal cell walls or inhibiting their replication.</p><p>The market analysis of antibiotics and antimycotics reveals a positive outlook for the industry. Factors driving market growth include the rising incidences of bacterial and fungal infections, increasing awareness about the benefits of early diagnosis and treatment, and the growing geriatric population.
