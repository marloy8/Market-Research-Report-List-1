<p><p>Integral concrete color is a type of colorant that is added to the concrete mixture during the mixing process. It is specifically designed to provide a consistent and uniform color throughout the entire depth of the concrete, rather than just on the surface. This is different from surface concrete color, which is applied after the concrete has been poured and finished.</p><p>The integral concrete color market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for aesthetically pleasing and decorative concrete in various construction projects, such as residential and commercial buildings, as well as in infrastructure development.</p><p>Integral concrete color provides a range of benefits, including enhanced durability, resistance to fading and chipping, and the ability to hide minor blemishes or imperfections in the concrete. These advantages make it a popular choice among architects, contractors, and homeowners.</p><p>Additionally, integral concrete color offers a wide variety of color options, allowing for customization and creative design choices.
