<p><p>Laser Indirect Ophthalmoscopes are medical devices used by ophthalmologists to examine and treat eye conditions. There are three types of these devices: Photocoagulating Laser Indirect Ophthalmoscope, which uses laser technology to seal leaking blood vessels in the retina; Photo Disrupting Laser Indirect Ophthalmoscope, which uses laser pulses to break down abnormal tissues, like tumors; and Photo Ablating Laser Indirect Ophthalmoscope, which uses laser energy to remove specific layers of tissue, such as scar tissue. These advanced ophthalmoscopes provide precise and effective treatments for a range of eye disorders.</p></p>
