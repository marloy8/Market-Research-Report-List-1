<p><p>Soya flour is a type of flour derived from soybeans, which are widely grown and used across the globe. It is obtained by grinding roasted soybeans into a fine powder. The increasing demand for plant-based protein sources, driven by the rising awareness of health benefits associated with plant-based diets, is a major factor driving the growth of the soya flour market. The versatility of soya flour also contributes to its market expansion, as it can be used as a substitute for wheat flour in baking, added to smoothies, or used as a thickening agent in various food products.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising prevalence of gluten intolerance and celiac disease among consumers has fueled the demand for gluten-free alternatives, including soya flour. Moreover, soya flour provides several health benefits, such as reducing cholesterol levels, promoting cardiovascular health, and preventing certain types of cancer. These factors have further propelled the market growth.</p><p>The latest trends in the soya flour market include the introduction of innovative soya flour-based products, such as gluten-free bakery items, snacks, and pasta. Additionally, the rising popularity of veganism and plant-based diets has led to an increased demand for soya flour in plant-based meat substitutes and dairy alternatives.</p><p>Overall, the soya flour market is poised to witness significant growth in the coming years, with a projected compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13.2% during the forecast period.</p></p>

<p><p>Soya flour is available in various market types based on its fat content. Low-fat soya flour has a reduced fat content, making it suitable for individuals seeking a healthier option. Defatted soya flour has the lowest fat content as the majority of fat has been removed, resulting in a higher protein concentration. Each market type caters to different preferences and dietary requirements, offering versatility in cooking and baking applications.</p></p>
