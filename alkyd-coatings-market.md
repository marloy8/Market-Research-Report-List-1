<p><p>Alkyd coatings refer to a group of paints that contain alkyd resins as a binding agent. This type of coating is categorized into three main types: non-drying, drying, and semi-drying coatings. Non-drying coatings do not harden and stay tacky, making them suitable for applications where adhesion is required. Drying coatings undergo a chemical reaction upon exposure to air, forming a solid film over time. Semi-drying coatings offer a balance between non-drying and drying coatings, as they become touch-dry but may still have some tackiness. These different types cater to various applications and desired characteristics within the alkyd coatings market.</p></p>
