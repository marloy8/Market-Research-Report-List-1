<p><p>Gasoline Automotive Injector Market Types refers to the different injection systems used in gasoline engines. Port Fuel Injection (PFI) is a type where fuel is injected into the intake port, mixing with the incoming air before reaching the combustion chamber. Direct Injection (DI) is a system where fuel is directly injected into the combustion chamber, resulting in better fuel atomization and combustion efficiency. PFI is commonly used in older vehicles, while DI offers improved performance, efficiency, and reduced emissions, and is increasingly used in modern vehicles.</p></p>
