<p><p>Air-cooled turbogenerators are classified into two main types: 2-pole and 4-pole generators. 2-pole air-cooled generators have a higher rotational speed compared to 4-pole generators. They are designed to run at a higher frequency, typically 3,000 rpm in order to produce electricity. On the other hand, 4-pole air-cooled generators have a lower rotational speed, usually around 1,500 rpm. They are designed to run at a lower frequency, typically 50 Hz or 60 Hz, making them suitable for various applications in industries and power plants.</p></p>
