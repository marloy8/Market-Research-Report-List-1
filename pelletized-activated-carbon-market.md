<p><p>Pelletized activated carbon is a form of activated carbon that is compressed into small cylindrical pellets. Key factors driving this growth include increasing awareness about water and air purification, growing industrialization and urbanization, and stringent environmental regulations. The use of pelletized activated carbon is expanding across various industries such as water treatment, air purification, food and beverage, pharmaceutical, and automotive.</p><p>In the water treatment sector, the demand for high-performing adsorbents to remove contaminants such as chlorine, odor, color, and taste is driving the adoption of pelletized activated carbon. In the air purification segment, the increasing focus on improving indoor air quality is boosting the usage of pelletized activated carbon filters. Similarly, in the food and beverage industry, pelletized activated carbon is used for decolorization, deodorization, and purification purposes.</p><p>The latest trends in the pelletized activated carbon market include advancements in pelletization technologies, such as the development of unique binder materials to improve the physical strength and durability of the pellets.
