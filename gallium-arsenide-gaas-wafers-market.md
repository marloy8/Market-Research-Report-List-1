<p><p>The Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) Wafer market consists of two main types: LEC (Liquid Encapsulated Czochralski) grown GaAs and VGF (Vertical Gradient Freeze) grown GaAs. LEC grown GaAs wafers are produced by slowly pulling a seed crystal from a molten GaAs solution, resulting in lower defects and better crystal quality. VGF grown GaAs wafers are created by gradually solidifying molten GaAs from a cooled crucible, leading to increased crystal purity and higher yield. These different growth methods cater to diverse applications and customer requirements in the GaAs wafer market.</p></p>
