<p><p>The spinal operating tables market is divided into two types: motorized spinal operating tables and non-motorized spinal operating tables. Motorized tables are equipped with electrically powered mechanisms that allow for easy adjustment and positioning of patients during spinal surgeries. These tables offer precise control and flexibility to surgeons. On the other hand, non-motorized tables are manually adjusted and rely on the physical strength of the surgical team. Although they may require more effort to operate, non-motorized tables are often more affordable and suitable for smaller healthcare facilities or lower-budget settings.</p></p>
