<p><p>A Fiber-Optic Current Sensor (FOCS) is a type of sensor that utilizes the principles of electromagnetic induction to measure electric current. It consists of an optical fiber cable wrapped around the conductor that carries the current. When current flows through the conductor, it generates a magnetic field that affects the properties of the light transmitted through the fiber. By measuring these changes, the FOCS can accurately determine the magnitude of the current.</p><p>The Fiber-Optic Current Sensor Market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for current measurement solutions in various industries such as power generation, oil and gas, and transportation is a key driver for market growth. FOCS offers advantages over traditional current sensors, such as immunity to electromagnetic interference, higher accuracy, and the ability to operate in harsh environments.</p><p>Additionally, the growing adoption of renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power, is fueling the demand for FOCS. These technologies often require accurate current measurement for power grid stability and management, and FOCS provides a reliable solution in these applications.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in fiber-optic technology, such as the development of low-cost and compact FOCS, are expected to drive market expansion.
