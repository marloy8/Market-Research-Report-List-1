<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Conventional seed markets are categorized into two types: herbicide-tolerance (HT) and insect-resistance (IR). HT seeds are engineered to be tolerant to specific herbicides, allowing farmers to control weeds effectively without harming the crop. These seeds enable farmers to use herbicides more efficiently and reduce the labor and cost associated with weed control. On the other hand, IR seeds are designed to produce crops that are resistant to certain insects, thereby reducing the need for pesticides and minimizing crop damage caused by pests. Both HT and IR seeds offer effective solutions for farmers to enhance crop yield and quality while optimizing input costs.</p></p>
