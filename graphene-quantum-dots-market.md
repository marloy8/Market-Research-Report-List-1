<p><p>Graphene quantum dots are nanoparticles made from graphene, a single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice. These dots have unique electronic and optical properties due to their size and quantum confinement effects. They are known for their high stability, good biocompatibility, and tunable bandgap, making them suitable for applications in various fields such as electronics, energy, and biotechnology.</p><p>The future outlook of the graphene quantum dots market is promising. The increasing demand for energy-efficient and high-performance electronic devices is driving the adoption of graphene quantum dots in the electronics industry. These dots are used in displays, sensors, transistors, and batteries, offering enhanced performance and longer lifespan compared to traditional materials. Moreover, the growing interest in renewable energy sources is further boosting the demand for graphene quantum dots in photovoltaic devices and energy storage systems.</p><p>In the healthcare sector, graphene quantum dots have shown great potential in diagnostic imaging, drug delivery, and cancer therapy. Their biocompatibility and ability to pass through cell membranes make them ideal candidates for targeted drug delivery systems.
