The market is segmented into three types: fluorocarbons, inorganic, and hydrocarbons/natural. Fluorocarbons, such as hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs) and chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), are synthetic compounds commonly used in HVACR systems. Inorganic substances, like ammonia and carbon dioxide, are non-flammable and have lower environmental impacts. Hydrocarbons, such as propane and butane, are natural substances with minimal greenhouse gas emissions. These market types represent different options for stakeholders to consider in terms of climate impact, safety, and regulatory compliance.</p></p>
