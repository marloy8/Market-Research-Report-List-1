<p><p>The On-board Emissions Measurement System (OEMS) is a device designed to measure the emissions produced by vehicles in real-world driving conditions. It is installed onboard the vehicle and captures data on pollutants such as carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, hydrocarbons, and particulate matter emitted during operation.</p><p>The global OEMS market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10% during the forecast period. The growing concern over air pollution, stringent emission regulations imposed by governments, and the need for accurate emission measurement are the key factors driving the market growth.</p><p>The market analysis indicates that the demand for OEMS is primarily driven by the automotive sector. With the rising number of vehicles on the road, there is an increasing need to monitor and control emissions levels. This has led to the integration of OEMS in vehicles to ensure compliance with emission norms. In addition, OEMS find applications in research and development activities, environmental monitoring, and emission testing, further fueling market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the OEMS market is the adoption of advanced technologies such as remote sensing and portable emission measurement systems (PEMS). Remote sensing technology allows for the measurement of emissions from vehicles while they are in motion, providing real-time data on their pollution levels. PEMS, on the other hand, enables portable and accurate emission measurement, making it suitable for on-road testing and inspection programs.</p><p>Furthermore, there is an increasing focus on developing OEMS that can measure greenhouse gas emissions. Governments worldwide are imposing strict regulations to reduce carbon emissions, driving the demand for advanced emission measurement systems that can accurately measure CO2 and other greenhouse gases.</p><p>Overall, the OEMS market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years, driven by the increasing need for emission measurement and control. Technological advancements and the integration of advanced features in OEMS are likely to further propel market expansion.</p></p>
