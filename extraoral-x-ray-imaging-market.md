<p><p>Extraoral X-ray imaging is a diagnostic imaging technique used in dentistry to capture images of the jaw and skull. Unlike intraoral X-ray imaging, which captures images inside the mouth, extraoral X-ray imaging captures images from outside the mouth. It provides a broad view of the maxillofacial region, allowing dentists to diagnose and monitor various dental and oral conditions. Extraoral X-ray imaging is widely used for orthodontic and oral surgery planning, as well as for detecting and assessing temporomandibular joint disorders.</p><p>The global market for extraoral X-ray imaging is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.5% during the forecast period. <p><p>The extraoral X-ray imaging market consists of two main types: panoramic and CBCT imaging. Panoramic X-rays provide a broad view of the entire oral cavity, including both upper and lower jaws. This type of imaging is commonly used in dental clinics for general oral health assessment. On the other hand, Cone Beam Computed Tomography (CBCT) is a three-dimensional imaging technique that provides detailed and accurate images of teeth, bone structures, and soft tissues. CBCT is often used in specialized dental procedures such as implants, orthodontics, and oral surgery.</p></p>
