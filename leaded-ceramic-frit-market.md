<p><p>Leaded ceramic frit is categorized into two market types: Type I and Type II. Type I refers to an opaque or translucent frit that is primarily used in the production of vitreous or opaque decorative items such as tiles or tableware. It offers high resistance to chemical reactions and is commonly employed in high-fired applications. On the other hand, Type II pertains to transparent or slightly colored frit used in the manufacturing of glassy or translucent products like glass sheets and screen-printing applications. This type demonstrates improved color stability and is often utilized in low-temperature processes.</p></p>
