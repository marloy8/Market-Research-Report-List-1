<p><p>Fire resistant glass is a type of glass that is designed to resist heat and inhibit the spread of fire. It is made by combining multiple layers of glass with fire-resistant interlayers, which can be made of materials like intumescent, liquid resin, or gel. When exposed to heat or fire, the interlayers expand and form an insulating char layer that prevents the glass from breaking or transmitting heat. Fire resistant glass is used in various applications such as doors, windows, partitions, facades, and roof glazing to provide fire protection and ensure the safety of buildings and occupants.</p><p>According to market analysis, the fire resistant glass market is projected to experience significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The fire-resistant glass market offers various types of glass that provide protection against fire-related hazards. Laminated fire-resistant glass consists of multiple layers of glass and interlayer films that hold the glass together when exposed to fire. Ceramic fire-resistant glass is created by embedding a transparent ceramic into the glass, offering thermal insulation. Tempered fire-resistant glass undergoes a heat treatment process that increases its strength and heat resistance. Wired fire-resistant glass contains a wire mesh embedded within the glass, adding strength and preventing it from shattering when exposed to heat.</p></p>
