<p><p>Dye intermediates refer to the chemical compounds that are used as raw materials in the production of dyes. There are different types of dye intermediates in the market, including mono chloro benzene based dye intermediates, monbenzidine based dye intermediates, MPD based dye intermediates, and other types. </p><p>Mono chloro benzene based dye intermediates are derived from mono chloro benzene and are commonly used in the manufacturing of azo dyes. Monbenzidine based dye intermediates, on the other hand, are produced from monbenzidine and are used in the production of disperse dyes. MPD based dye intermediates are derived from MPD and are used in the production of acid dyes.</p><p>Apart from these specific types, there are also other dye intermediates in the market that are not categorized under the aforementioned types. These could include various compounds used in the production of different types of dyes, such as reactive dyes, vat dyes, or direct dyes.</p></p>
